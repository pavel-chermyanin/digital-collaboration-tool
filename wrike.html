What is Wrike?

A Powerful Project Management Tool

  • True Collaboration

    Wrike promotes true collaboration within the teams. It helps the teams turn every chat, meeting and brainstorm into plans that will yield results. Teams can collaborate on tasks through the live editing option. It guarantees that everyone is in the loop, and they can contribute to any changes that ought to be made.

  • Awesome Features

    Users will enjoy the built-in approval and proofing features that eradicate infinite feedback loops. Wrike ensures that teams can attain the full context of the feedback to guarantee that they will get everything right. In return, they can utilise project deliverables like PDFs, videos and images to provide the best results.

  • Customize and Track

    Wrike's custom request option ensures that teams can have all the necessary information before starting a project. It guarantees that there won't be frequent questions about the best way forward. Your team can have the information and the resources necessary to produce the best products and services. Managers can track the performance of their teams with ease since Wrike provides them with dynamic and visual reports. The information from Wrike Analyze makes it easier for the manager to expound to the stakeholders.

Wrike has been able to offer its users some unique features and content. Some of the most used features include custom workflows, custom fields, requests, user groups, Wrike Reports and project templates. The custom workflows enable teams to go through a life cycle efficiently. Therefore, they can create custom workflows to ensure that all the cycles within the project are completed on time, and each member of the team knows which part of the process they have to contribute to.

On the other hand, the custom fields provide teams with the ability to track unique data points. These fields are crucial when tracking information, filtering and reporting on any data related to the tasks. The request option ensures that there is a standard procedure for teams to attain any incoming work. It’s easy to create request forms and gather any additional suggestions for attaining more tasks.

Managers can create user groups for teams and departments to share projects, folders and tasks quickly. Users can easily focus on one task and proceed to the next one after sharing it with the other teams within the organisation. The reports section provides users with the ability to gather accurate data about the projects. Teams can share information and create reports on their progress and everything they used to achieve their results. They achieve this using the project templates, which are ideal for small and mid-sized projects.

Get Wrike

You can choose between lots of account types for your team, the first being the free version. A free account is ideal for trying out Wrike and learning about all the different features. It’s also perfect for small teams since it supports up to five members and unlimited collaborators. The collaborators can view and discuss the projects.

However, with free Wrike you can’t edit and create tasks. There aren’t any limitations on the number of projects to create, but there is a 2GB storage space cap. If and when this is not enough for you, there’s also the professional option. This version is the best for collaborating and planning projects. The professional account provides users with everything in the free version and adds Gannt charts, subtasks, collaborators, shareable dashboards, and limited integration options.

The Wrike professionalv version has a 5GB cap and a monthly 15GB option for videos. The other options include Wrike for Marketers, Wrike for Professional Services Performance, Wrike for Marketers Performance, Enterprise, and Wrike for Professional Services. All of these are useful for project management and digital facilitation

All these account types provide the user with similar view options. These view options enable your team members to view the effort of everyone across the team. In return, it’s easier to understand how much time a team spends completing the projects or tasks. Gannt provides users with an interactive interface where they can drag, slide and drop items. The Gannt chart assists your team members in reordering the tasks and scheduling when to complete them.
